Dhaka

Dhaka is the capital and largest city of Bangladesh. Situated on the banks of the Buriganga River, it is the political, economic, and cultural heart of the country. Known for its vibrant markets, historical architecture, and modern skyline, Dhaka attracts millions of visitors every year and serves as a hub for commerce and governance.

History

Dhaka's history dates back to the 7th century, with significant development during the Mughal period. The city became the capital of Bengal under Mughal rule in 1608. Historical sites like Lalbagh Fort and Ahsan Manzil reflect its rich heritage.

Geography and Climate

Dhaka is located in central Bangladesh, with flat terrain and a network of rivers. The climate is tropical, with hot, humid summers and mild winters. Flooding can occur during monsoon seasons.

Economy and Infrastructure

Dhaka is the commercial hub of Bangladesh, hosting industries such as textiles, IT, banking, and trade. Infrastructure development is ongoing, with rapid urbanization and expansion of roads, bridges, and metro systems.

Culture and Education

The city is home to universities, museums, cultural festivals, and religious sites. Dhaka showcases a rich mix of traditional Bengali culture and modern urban lifestyle. Local cuisine, music, and arts thrive across the city.
